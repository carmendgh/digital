PRODUCT DESIGN – UX, UI


Accenture – Interactive Quiz

The brief
As one of the world’s leading consulting and technology companies, Accenture's job is to help organisations solve their toughest challenges by designing innovative solutions that improves the way the world works and lives. And they need powerful minds to help make that change happen. This was a chance for candidates to find out what powered their minds and where their strengths came into play.
The challenge
University campus was such a crowded space for brands as there are loads of different companies trying to attract early talent. Accenture was one of them. They wanted and installation to engage students at the on-campus fair – and we had suggested doing something a bit different this year, something that could take schemes deadlines and looking for jobs out of students' minds whilst learning about their offering.
The solution
We came up with Powerful Minds, a campaign created in order to attract graduates onto Accenture's entry level programmes. Among various initiatives, the campaign included a fun interactive quiz that would help students discover the inner workings of their mind and get them thinking about where their strengths might lie.

Users were split into 5 mind types based on their answers to the given questions: Adaptive, Action, Collaborative, Reflective and Analytical. We created a different visual identity for each of them, which was later on used across the graduate careers website to promote the different schemes.
I lead the UX, interaction and visual design for this quiz, which launched at the on-campus fair and would later on be integrated on Accenture's website for all online visitors. We created a set of 50 questions that would randomly be picked for each session. So, it didn't matter how many times someone wanted to do the quiz, they always had a different experience of it.

Once answered all questions, students would land on a landing page where they could learn more about the strengths of their mind type, meet current Accenture graduates that decided to take on the same path, and learn about the available programmes recommended to them.
The results
  • +1,400 completed questionnaires
  • +30,000 unique visitors to website
  • 150% website traffic increase from previous year
  • 17,9% campus attendance increase from previous year
